Patriotic Meatloaf

Nothing screams freedom, grit, and unapologetic American pride like a thick-cut slab of South Texas meatloaf, straight from the oven and packed with enough flavor to make bald eagles cry tears of joy. This isn’t some weak, cafeteria-style meatloaf—this is a beefy, chorizo-infused, mesquite-smoked masterpiece that could feed a ranch full of hardworking patriots. We’re talking Texas-raised beef, bold spices, and a glaze so rich and tangy it’ll have you saluting the flag before your first bite. With every forkful, you’ll taste the smoky essence of backyard BBQs, Friday night football, and the kind of down-home cooking that built this great nation. And just like the Alamo, this meatloaf stands its ground—thick, proud, and ready to take on anything.

Now, if you're ready to bring this star-spangled, gun-blazing, rocket-soaring, all-American beast to your kitchen, grab your apron and prepare for greatness. This recipe is simple but bold, just like the hardworking folks of Texas, and it'll have your taste buds singing the national anthem. Whether you're feeding a table of hungry patriots or just treating yourself to a true taste of home, this meatloaf is guaranteed to be the most American thing you've eaten all week. Ingredients

  • Ground Chuck This recipe calls for ground chuck, but some reviewers like to use other kinds of ground meat (like beef or venison).
  • Vegetables The nearly-pureed carrot, celery, onion, bell pepper, mushrooms, and garlic will keep the meatloaf moist and flavorful.
  • Worcestershire Sauce A tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce gives the meatloaf its umami-rich flavor.
  • Egg The egg helps bind the meatloaf together.
  • Spices Chef John uses dried Italian herbs, salt, pepper, and cayenne pepper to season his meatloaf.
  • Bread Crumbs Bread crumbs will bind the meatloaf together.
  • Glaze This meatloaf has a glaze made with brown sugar, ketchup, Dijon mustard, and sriracha.
